    • Make a fort.

    Making a fort is almost never a bad idea. They are fun to construct, play games in, watch a movie in, or making up an imaginary world in. It's perfect in the living room on a rainy day, and great for outside when it's nice.You might think forts are for young kids only but we think you'd be surprised at the fun that can be had with a fort.

    Have a cook-off.

    You can go full-blown "Chopped" style or simply create a joint meal that you wouldn't have normally. Switch things up by thinking outside the box and crafting it snacks for a movie night or brunch on a Sunday!

    Teach your house-mates something new.

    There's something amazing about learning new things. Almost everyone has something they love or know a lot about that someone else doesn't. Have everyone spend a little bit of time making an engaging presentation that you all can take turns spreading some knowledge with. You learn a little more about the people that you live with and you can gain some cool new knowledge too!

    Have a board or card game tournament.

    Create a bracket for the people that you live with and battle it out to see who the best is! Make a whole day thing or do it over the course of a week when everyone can get together and engage in some friendly competition.

    Have a spirit week.

    Highscool does it right. Keep up what you would be doing in school or reminisce on the days from highschool. Make a fun or thunky theme for each day and have the people you live with dress up and do activities that go along with it!
